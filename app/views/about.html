I am a first year graduate student in the robotics program at Oregon State University. I am researching in the Autonomous Systems and Distributed Intelligence Lab with Professor Kagan Tumer as my adviser. Prior to starting my PhD, I graduated with a computer science degree from MIT and worked in Manhattan for a year.
My research is currently focused on the coordination of agents in tightly coupled systems with only local information through reward shaping and mechanism design. In plain English, I am creating incentive structures to encourage robots to work together on tasks that require multiple agents (like picking up a heavy table).
My general academic interests are in machine learning and robotics, but I also am very interested in functional programming. Almost all of my individual research and project code is written in Haskell, and I can say that it has made writing and maintaining my software much more enjoyable.

Outside of academics, I enjoy Taekwondo, Karaoke, and Board Games.
